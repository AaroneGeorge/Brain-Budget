● Here's my analysis — focused on the leverage points and second-order dynamics most builders walking into this
won't notice. I dug through the hackathon page, the 1Shot technical brief, the x402/ERC-7710 specs, and the
previous MetaMask Cook-Off winners to find the patterns.

First, the thing nobody tells you: this is a prize-stacking game, not a "build one great app" game

The naïve read is "$14,000, I'll build something cool and aim for first." The structural read is very different:

- There are 7 prize buckets and 17 individual payouts. A single project can legitimately win multiple tracks at
once. The Venice track explicitly requires you to first qualify for a main track — so it's a multiplier on top of
another submission, not a separate build. Same with the 1Shot Relayer track ("open to all tracks").
- So one well-architected project = an autonomous agent that does x402 payments via redelegation, runs through
the 1Shot relayer, and uses Venice for its reasoning = is eligible for Best Agent + Best x402+7710 + Best A2A +
Best Venice + Best 1Shot Relayer simultaneously. That's up to $5,500+ from one codebase. Almost nobody designs
for this; they pick one track.

The hidden corollary: "Judges reserve the right to withhold a track prize if quality standards aren't met." Read
that as a market signal. It means thin/empty tracks pay out to the only competent entry. Which track is thinnest?
Keep reading.

The track EV ranking (where the money actually is)

Best A2A Coordination ($3,000) is the highest expected value. The 1Shot brief literally calls it "the most
technically ambitious track." That phrasing is a gift — ambitious = scary = few entrants = withholding clause
works in your favor. It requires redelegation (ERC-7710's permission-hopping: Agent A holds a delegation, narrows
it, and re-delegates a subset to Agent B without the user re-approving). Most hackathon teams won't even attempt
a working redelegation chain. If you can demo two agents passing a narrowed permission down a chain and
executing, you're likely competing against a handful of entries, not 100.

Best Agent ($3,000) has "intentional latitude" — the 1Shot brief says judges "want creative, well-executed ideas,
not just technically correct implementations." Translation: this is the design/storytelling track, and the bar
is product polish, not protocol depth. Different muscle than A2A.

Best x402 + ERC-7710 is the most crowded conceptually because x402 is hot right now — but here's the non-obvious
bit: real-world x402 demand is weak (CoinDesk in March 2026 reported the micropayment demand "is just not there
yet"). So a judge has probably seen ten "AI agent buys an API call" demos. A non-obvious payment use case (agent
paying another agent for a sub-task; streaming payroll; pay-per-inference where Venice is the thing being paid
for) stands out hard.

The free money: Best Social Media (5×$100) and Best Feedback (5×$100). These cost ~an hour. Document your build
journey on X tagging @MetaMaskDev, and write genuinely sharp feedback on the docs/DX. Most serious builders are
too proud to bother — that's exactly why the field is shallow. $200 for thread + feedback writeup is the best
hourly rate in the event.

The technical distinction that trips everyone: 7715 vs 7710 vs 7702

These get used interchangeably and it costs people qualification. Get this right and you'll out-execute most
teams:

- ERC-7715 = the request side. A dapp asks a wallet for a permission ("let me spend 10 USDC/day"). This is
wallet_grantPermissions.
- ERC-7710 = the redemption/delegation side. It's how a smart contract account holds and exercises/forwards that
permission — the "delegation manager" layer. Redelegation lives here.
- EIP-7702 = the account-upgrade mechanism. Turns a plain EOA into a smart account temporarily so it can do any
of the above. This is what 1Shot's relayer uses to "upgrade EOAs to smart accounts through the relayer."

A2A coordination is fundamentally a 7710 redelegation story. The Agent track is usually a 7715 request story. If
your demo video doesn't clearly show the right one in the main flow, you can get bounced — the rules twice
emphasize "in the main flow, not bolted on / peripheral integrations won't qualify."

The two cheat codes the organizers handed you

1. 1Shot published a "relayer skill" for agentic IDEs (Cursor, Copilot, Claude). Most people will hand-roll
relayer integration and burn a day on it. Pull their skill and you compress that to minutes. (I'm Claude Code — I
can wire that skill into this project for you.)
2. The judges told you the answer for the 1Shot track: "relayer webhooks for transaction status updates over
polling methods." That is a literal grading rubric leak. Implement webhook-driven tx status (not a polling loop)
and you've satisfied the stated preference. Trivial to do, and almost everyone defaults to polling because it's
easier to demo.

What past winners reveal — and the trap in it

Every standout from the prior MetaMask Smart Accounts Cook-Offs was a subscription/recurring-payments play:
SubPay Automator, Autiv, TipStream, all "gasless recurring payments." That tells you two things:

- Subscriptions are the proven judge-pleaser for smart-account delegation (it's the canonical "approve once,
charge repeatedly under limits" story). Safe.
- It's now saturated. A fourth subscription dApp is a yawn. The winning move this round is to take that proven
mechanic (delegated recurring spend under limits) and point it at the new primitives this event added:
agent-to-agent and x402. E.g., not "user subscribes to a creator" but "an agent subscribes to another agent's
service and pays per-call via x402, under a redelegated budget cap." Same trusted skeleton, novel surface.

Where Venice AI is genuinely a weapon, not a checkbox

Venice's differentiator vs OpenAI/Anthropic APIs is privacy-first + uncensored + no-retention + 200+ open models,
API-compatible. The lazy move is "I call Venice instead of GPT for a chatbot." The judges said it must be "core"
and produce "meaningful AI-powered output." The non-obvious angle: build something that requires the properties
only Venice offers —

- An autonomous financial/trading agent where you genuinely don't want prompts/strategy logged by a centralized
provider (privacy + no-retention is the point, and it pairs thematically with self-custody/smart accounts).
- An agent that needs uncensored reasoning over on-chain/financial content other APIs refuse to touch.
- Venice as the thing being paid for via x402 — agent pays per inference through the relayer. That single design
choice qualifies you for x402 + Venice + 1Shot tracks simultaneously.

Privacy-preserving AI + self-custodial accounts is a coherent narrative, and a coherent narrative is what wins
the "Best Agent" latitude track.

My recommended single-project architecture (maximizes track overlap)

▎ An autonomous "agent that hires sub-agents." A user grants a top-level smart account a budget via 7715. A
▎ coordinator agent redelegates narrowed budgets (7710) to specialist sub-agents. Each sub-agent pays for
▎ services (or pays Venice for inference) per-call via x402, with gas settled in stablecoins through the 1Shot
▎ permissionless relayer using 7702 + webhook status. Venice does the actual planning/reasoning, privately.
That one build is eligible for A2A + Agent + x402 + Venice + 1Shot relayer — 5 tracks, up to ~$5,500 — and it's a
genuinely novel demo because it chains redelegation, which almost no one will get working.

Risks / watch-outs

- The demo video is a hard gate. Rules repeat that the video must show the Smart Accounts Kit working in the main
flow. Budget real time for a clean, narrated screen recording — many technically strong projects get withheld
here.
- Testnet vs mainnet: the 1Shot relayer track says "mainnet relayer" and "7702 authorizations." Confirm which
chain (likely Base/Linea/Sepolia variants) and whether mainnet stablecoin gas is required vs testnet — this is
the kind of detail that disqualifies otherwise-great entries. Verify in the workshop recordings.
- Timeline reality: registration closes Jun 15, 2026 and that's also the submission deadline (10:59 UTC). It's 4
days from today (Jun 11). The redelegation chain is the long pole — if you want A2A, start there today and treat
x402/Venice as additive.
